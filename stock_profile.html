Quick Note

  • Bid: the highest price a buyer will pay to buy a specified number of shares of a stock at any given time.

  • Ask-Bid Spread: difference between ask (offer/sell) price and bid (purchase/buy) price of a security.

  • Bull: a bull market is a market condition where investors are expecting prices to rise.

  • Bear: a bear market is a market condition where investors are expecting prices to fall.

  • Limit Order: a type of order which executes at the price placed for buy or sell.

  • Market Order: a type of order which executes as quickly as possible at the market price.

  • Day Order: a direction to a broker to execute a trade at a specific price that expires at the end of trading day if it is not completed.

  • Volatility: how fast a stock moves up or down.

  • Going Long: betting on the stock's price will increase so that you can buy low and sell high.

  • Averaging Down: when an investor buys as the stock goes down so as to decrease the price at which purchased.

  • Capitalization: what market thinks a company's value is.

  • Float: the number of shares which can be actually traded after deducting the shares held by insiders.

  • Authorized Shares: total number of shares that a company can trade.

  • IPO: Initial Public Offering; happens when the private company becomes a publicly traded company.

  • Secondary Offering: another offering in order to sell more stocks and to raise more money from the public.

  • Dividend: portion of the company's earnings which is paid to the shareholders.

  • Broker: a person who buy or sell stocks on your behalf.

  • Exchange: a place where different types of investment are traded.

  • Portfolio: a collection of investments owned by you.

  • Margin: margin account lets a person to borrow money from the broker to buy shares.

  • Sector: a group of stocks in the same sector.

  • Stock Symbol: a one to three character alphabet root symbol which represents a company listed on the exchange.
